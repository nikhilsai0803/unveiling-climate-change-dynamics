🌍 Global Average Prediction

Predicts the average temperature across all of Earth's land surface — the same metric used by climate scientists and the IPCC.

⚠ What does "Global Average" mean?
The global land average is computed by averaging ALL land points on Earth — including the Sahara (+45°C), Arctic tundra (−30°C), Siberia (−20°C) and tropics (+35°C). The result is always between 5°C–14°C, regardless of local conditions. This is a climate science metric, not a local weather number.

Understanding the Two Modes

City vs Global — What's the Difference?

📍

City Prediction

Trained on local baselines per city. Hyderabad May → ~36–38°C. Moscow January → ~-7°C. Useful for real-world applications, travel planning, agricultural forecasting.

🌍

Global Average

Predicts mean across all land on Earth. Always 5–14°C. Used by IPCC, climate scientists, and policy makers to track warming trends and anomalies relative to pre-industrial baseline.

Live Weather Layer

Open-Meteo API provides real-time temperature for city predictions. Free, no API key required. Works on all hosting platforms. 10,000 req/day.
