About This Project

Unveiling Climate Change Dynamics Through Earth Surface Temperature Analysis

A full-stack data science project combining classical machine learning, exploratory data analysis, and a modern web interface to make climate data accessible and actionable.

🎯

Problem Statement

Climate change is the defining challenge of our era, yet temperature data remains inaccessible to most people. This project bridges that gap by transforming raw CSV records into interactive insights and forward-looking predictions.

✅

Project Goals

Identify long-term warming trends. Visualise seasonal and decadal patterns. Build interpretable ML models. Provide an interactive prediction interface. Enable reproducibility via a detailed Jupyter notebook.

Data Source

Berkeley Earth Surface Temperature Dataset

The dataset used is the Berkeley Earth Global Temperature record (also available on Kaggle as GlobalTemperatures.csv). It contains monthly land average temperatures from 1750 to 2015, with columns for average, minimum, and maximum land temperatures as well as land-ocean combined averages.

1750–2015 Monthly resolution Land + Ocean Global average Berkeley Earth
